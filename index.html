DESCRIPTION

Ediz Ucar is a 23-year-old software engineer based in the UK, currently working at Entrust on HSMs and cybersecurity-flavoured infrastructure.

Outside of work, he spends a non-trivial amount of time thinking about mechanistic interpretability and how sparse autoencoders behave inside LLMs, along with Karl Popper-flavoured epistemology (falsifiability, critical discussion, the usual suspects).

WORK

Entrust — Software Engineer
Hands-on with hardware security modules, key management, and the general business of making cryptographic operations work reliably in the real world.
Mechanistic interpretability @ home
Building and analysing tools for understanding internal representations in language models, with a focus on sparse autoencoders and how features evolve as activations flow through layers.

RESEARCH

Interested in how features in LLMs transform across layers and how sparse autoencoders can be used to trace and steer them. Co-author on:

  • Evolution of SAE Features Across Layers in LLMs — analysing statistical relationships between SAE features across successive layers of a transformer and visualising their evolution.
    link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.08869

PHILOSOPHY

Broadly sympathetic to Popperian critical rationalism: knowledge as conjecture and refutation, arguments surviving only to the extent they withstand attempted falsification.

In practice this looks like: strong opinions, weak identifiers; tests over vibes; and an instinctive preference for transparent mechanisms over opaque authority.

INTERESTS

  • Mechanistic interpretability for language models.
  • Sparse autoencoders, feature graphs, and activation-level debugging.
  • Applied cryptography, HSMs, and key management.
  • Popper, critical discussion, and better epistemics.
